The first step to starting a business is to write a well-considered, comprehensive business plan. First, you can learn more about [how to create a business plan](/pages/create-a-business-plan).

The State of New Jersey also offers other services that can help you write a business plan. These include:

- [New Jersey Small Business Development Centers](https://njsbdc.com/): NJSBDC has regional offices that cover every county in NJ. They provide free (taxpayer supported) assistance to entrepreneurs and can help you write a business plan.
- [SCORE](https://www.score.org/): Score also offers assistance to NJ entrepreneurs. SCORE is staffed by volunteers, and each chapter has a less formalized process than NJSBDC.
- [Women’s Center for Entrepreneurship](https://www.wcecnj.org/) (Northern New Jersey): The WCE services northern New Jersey and can help you write a business plan. The WCE's services are not strictly for women entrepreneurs.
- [Latin American Economic Development Association](https://newjersey.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/www.laeda.com) (Southern New Jersey): LAEDA services southern New Jersey and can help you write a business plan. LAEDA's services are not strictly for Latin American entrepreneurs.
- [Union County Economic Development Corporation](https://ucedc.com/): The UCEDC provides free classes and paid business mentoring services and can help you create a business plan.
