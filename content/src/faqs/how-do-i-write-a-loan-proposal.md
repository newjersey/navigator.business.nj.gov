<p id=""><strong id="">How to Write a Loan Proposal</strong>

<p id="">A loan approval request depends on how well the businessperson presents themselves, the business and its financial needs to a lender. Remember, lenders want to make loans but they want to make good loans, loans they know will be repaid. The best way to improve your chances of obtaining a loan is to prepare a written loan proposal. A good loan proposal will contain the following key elements:

<ul id=""><li id="">General Information</li><li id="">Provide the business name, address, names of principals and the social security number of each principal.</li><li id="">State the purpose of the loan and provide exactly what the loan will be used for and why it is needed.</li><li id="">Provide the amount required in the exact amount needed to achieve your purpose.</li><li id="">Business Description</li><li id="">Give the history and nature of the business with details of the business’s age, number of employees and current business assets.</li><li id="">Provide details on the ownership structure (the company’s business structure).</li><li id="">Management Profile</li><li id="">Develop a management profile detailing the responsibility of each principal staff member.</li><li id="">Include the individual’s background, education, experience, skills and accomplishments.</li><li id="">Market Information</li><li id="">Provide a clear definition of the products and markets.</li><li id="">Identify competition and explain how the business competes in the marketplace.</li><li id="">Profile the company’s customers and explain how the business can satisfy their needs.</li><li id="">Financial Information</li><li id="">Provide financial statements including balance sheets and income statements for the past three years. If just starting out, provide a projected balance sheet and income statement.</li><li id="">Prepare a personal financial statement on yourself and other principal owners of the business.</li><li id="">List all collateral that could be pledged to the bank as security for the loan.</li>
<p id=""><strong id="">How a Loan Request Is Reviewed</strong>

<p id="">A loan officer’s primary concern when reviewing a loan request is whether or not the loan will be repaid. To help answer this question, many loan officers will order a copy of your business credit report. Using the credit report and the information you have provided, the lending officer will consider the following:

<ul id=""><li id="">Have the principal(s) invested savings or personal equity in the business totaling at least 25 percent to 50 percent of the loan requested? Remember a lender or investor will not finance 100 percent of the business.</li><li id="">Does the principal(s) have a sound record of credit worthiness as indicated by your credit report, work history and letters of recommendations? This is very important.</li><li id="">Does the principal(s) have sufficient experience and training to operate a successful business?</li><li id="">Has the principal(s) prepared a loan proposal and business plan that’s demonstrates an understanding of the business and commitment to the success of the business?</li><li id="">Does the business have sufficient “cash flow” to make the monthly payments on the loan request?</li>
