  No one will face a gas, water, or electric utility disconnection before December 31st, 2021. 

  **Businesses **that need help paying utility bills or arrears should contact their utility company about assistance programs, deferments, and payment plans, or visit the New Jersey Board of Public Utilities' [Assistance Programs page](https://urldefense.com/v3/__https:/t.e2ma.net/click/jk9xkd/33ls2aq/7iwxqk__;!!J30X0ZrnC1oQtbA!absB27GgS7SEMShAV9HO8o40w0S6r-VHtTGxs2OEYoyhONQGeuqzqjId1XYUkZLmHX4jbA$) for information about programs that can help.

  ‍
  A handful of programs are available to help you and your employees **pay residential utilities**. Consider utilizing and sharing these resources with your employees:

  * [Universal Service Fund](https://www.nj.gov/dca/divisions/dhcr/offices/usf.html) (lowers the amount you have to pay for utilities)
  * [USF-Fresh Start](http://www.nj.gov/bpu/assistance/programs) (provides debt forgiveness for utilities)
  * [Payment Assistance for Gas and Electric](https://njpoweron.org/page/) (offers payment assistance for utilities)
  * [Emergency Rental Assistance](https://njdca.onlinepha.com/) (covers utilities included in rental costs)
  * [Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program](https://www.nj.gov/dca/divisions/dhcr/offices/hea.html) (gives financial assistance for utilities)

  The[ Utility Customer’s Bill of Rights](https://www.nj.gov/bpu/assistance/rights/) (outlines the laws around customer protections) 

  For additional help, the Department of Community Affairs has a [variety of programs](http://nj.gov/dca/dcaid) that can help with rent costs, eviction prevention, and more.**
