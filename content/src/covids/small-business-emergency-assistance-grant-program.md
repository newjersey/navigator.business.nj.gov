The NJEDA is currently processing applications and will notify you of any updates by email. If you have questions about your application, call 844-965-1125.\n\n### Make Sure Your Businesses is Properly Registered\n\nIn order to receive a Phase 4 grant, your business must be registered to do business in the State of New Jersey with the NJ Division of Taxation. If your business is not recognized by the Division of Taxation, you must provide a valid Business Registration Certificate. [You can check your business registration certificate status online](https://www1.state.nj.us/TYTR_BRC/jsp/BRCLoginJsp.jsp). \n\nIf your business is properly registered, you will receive immediate confirmation after entering your information. If your business is not properly registered, email [njdorescustomerservice@treas.nj.gov](http://njdorescustomerservice@treas.nj.gov) (allow up to 2 days for follow-up) or call 609-292-9292. You should state clearly that your request is connected to an application for the NJEDA’s Small Business Emergency Assistance Grant Program – Phase 4. Most issues can be resolved quickly.\n\nFor more information on the business registration process, watch one of the webinars on the topic the NJEDA hosted with the NJ Department of Treasury. Applications\n\n**Applications for the Small Business Emergency Assistance Grant Program – Phase 4 are closed. **\n\n### Awards\n\nGrant awards will be calculated based on the number of full-time equivalent employees (FTEs) businesses employ. Micro-businesses with five or fewer FTEs and sole proprietorships will receive up to $10,000; businesses with six to 25 FTEs will receive up to $15,000; and businesses with 26 to 50 FTEs will receive up to$20,000. \_[Access the grant size estimator](http://forms.business.nj.gov/grant-4/size). \n\nIn line with the State's commitment to a stronger, fairer recovery, Phase 4 funding will be allocated to support the most adversely affected businesses, including restaurants, micro-businesses, and child care providers, as well as other small businesses. To ensure grants reach businesses in the hardest hit communities, including communities of color, one-third of funding will be targeted to businesses with a primary business location within the 715 census tracts designated as eligible to be selected as an Opportunity Zone.\n\n### **Frequently Asked Questions**\n\nAnswers to common questions about Phase 4 of the Small Business Emergency Assistance Grant Program are listed below in a variety of languages:\n\n* ‍[English](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-english/)[‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_spanish/)\n* [Spanish](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_spanish/)[‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-211_ptb/)\n* [Portuguese](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-211_ptb/)[‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_italian/)\n* [Italian](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_italian/)[‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_hindi/)\n* [Hindi](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_hindi/) [‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_gujarati/)\n* [Gujarati](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_gujarati/) [‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_cantonese/)\n* [Cantonese](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_cantonese/) [‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_arabic/)\n* [Arabic](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_arabic/) [‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_tagalog/)\n* [Tagalog](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_tagalog/) [‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_polish/)\n* [Polish](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_polish/) [‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_mandarin/)\n* [Mandarin](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_mandarin/)[‍](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_korean/)\n* [Korean](https://www.njeda.com/grant-phase-4-faqs-4-13-21_korean/)"
