When you form a corporation, your default tax status is C corp. After you register your business with the state, you can elect to be taxed as an S corp with the IRS by filing Form 2553. With S corp status, profits and losses pass through to shareholders' personal tax returns instead of being taxed at the business level. S corps are limited to 100 shareholders and one class of stock.

**S Corp Advantages** 

* Limited liability for business debts 
* Shareholders report their share of corporate profit or loss on their personal tax returns 
* Shareholders can use a corporate loss to offset income from other sources 

**S Corp Disadvantages** 

* Venture capitals aren't willing to fund S corps or any other pass-through entity 
* Income must be allocated to owners according to their ownership interests
