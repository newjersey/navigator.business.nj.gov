sub-heading-text: From alternate names to DBAs, find out everything you need to know about naming your business in New Jersey.
main-link-text: Name Your Business
main-text-1: Your business name is the name you will use to represent your business. The process of registering your business name differs depending on your business structure, also known as a legal structure.
heading-2: Search Available Business Names
main-text-2: "**If you are an LLC, C-Corp, S-Corp, LP, or LLP** you should check if your business name is available with the New Jersey Department of Treasury. If your name is available, you will reserve your name when you submit your business formation documents. You will need your business Certificate of Formation to officially register your business name.\_\n\n**If you are a sole proprietor or a general partnership** and you do not want to use your personal name as your business name, you can register a business name at your local clerk’s office.\n\nOnce you have found your business name, check out our [business registration guide](https://business.nj.gov/pages/register-your-business)."
link-text-2: Search Available Business Names
heading-3: Use an Alternate Business Name
main-text-3: After a business entity has been established or receives authorization to do business in New Jersey, it may legally do business under an alternate name once the alternate name is registered. For example, ABC Food and Beverage LLC could get an alternate name called John’s Pizza and put that on their storefront. Franchises often do this or businesses who want to publicly go by a different name.
link-text-3: Register an Alternate Name
heading-4: Doing Business As (DBA) for Out-of-State Business
main-text-4: "#### **Doing Business As (DBA) names are for out-of-state business ONLY.\_**\n\n***If your business is already registered in New Jersey and you’re looking to get an alternate name, please refer to the Alternate Name information above****.*\n\nAn out-of-state business that is looking to register in New Jersey must first check to see if the exact name that is on the formation document in your home state is available. Check if your business [name is available](https://www.njportal.com/DOR/BusinessNameSearch/Search/BusinessName)\_in New Jersey. If the exact name is available, that name MUST\_be used.\n\nIf your home state business name is not available in New Jersey, you need to establish a secondary or 'doing business as\" name for New Jersey purposes. [Search available DBA names](https://www.njportal.com/DOR/BusinessNameSearch/Search/BusinessName). Once an available name is found, you can register the DBA name when you form your business. \n\nLearn more about [out-of-state business registration.](https://business.nj.gov/pages/out-of-state-business-registration)"
heading-5: Reserve a Business Name
main-text-5: "If you reserve a business name with the State, you can “save” a name before you register your business. **Reserving a name is optional and not recommended.\_**\n\nReserved names are available for 6 months. To register your business with that name before the 6 months are over, you must mail a paper application.\n\n‍**Warning:** If you reserve a business name, you can’t use that same name to[ sign up in our online system](https://navigator.business.nj.gov/onboarding?page=1&_gl=1*1fxhkhc*_ga*MjA5MjA4NDc3Ni4xNzUxMzg2Mzkx*_ga_HL8LBD05DX*czE3NTUyNzk0NTIkbzk2JGcwJHQxNzU1Mjc5NDYwJGo1MiRsMCRoMA..). In addition, there is a lengthy process to cancel the name reservation, and you will have to pay a fee to cancel."
