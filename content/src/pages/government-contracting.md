heading-1: Requirements to Bid on New Jersey State Contracts
main-text-1: "To bid on New Jersey State contracts, most businesses must:\n\n* Register with the [Division of Revenue and Enterprise Services (DORES)](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/revenue/gettingregistered.shtml)\n* Get a [Business Registration Certificate (BRC)](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/revenue/busregcert.shtml). You don't need a BRC to place a bid, but you must have it before you start any work\n* Register with [NJSTART](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/purchase/vendor.shtml)\n* [Follow New Jersey laws and regulations\_](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/purchase/forms.shtml)\n\n### [‍](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/purchase/forms.shtml)NJSTART[‍](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/purchase/forms.shtml)\n\nThe Division of Purchase and Property (DPP) uses NJSTART for most State contracts. You can find requests for proposals (RFPs), active State contracts, and vendor information on [NJSTART](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/purchase/vendor.shtml). NJSTART can help you:\n\n* Find new bidding opportunities\n* Save and organize bidding documents\n* Track your business certifications and licenses\n* See active State contracts and vendor information\n* Receive payment for awarded contracts\n\n### Public Works Contracts\n\nBusinesses interested in bidding on public works contracts must register on the [New Jersey Wage Hub](https://njwages.nj.gov/). Public works are projects paid for by the State to build, fix, or maintain things like roads and buildings.\n\nThe New Jersey Wage Hub allows public works contractors to submit and manage their certified payroll records online. It helps contractors follow fair pay rules according to the [Prevailing Wage Act](https://www.nj.gov/labor/wageandhour/tools-resources/laws/prevailingwageact.shtml) and the [Diane B. Allen Equal Pay Act](https://www.nj.gov/labor/wageandhour/tools-resources/equal-pay/). All forms and records must be submitted online through the Wage Hub. [More information about public works, prevailing wage, and certified payroll requirements is available on the NJ Department of Labor's website.](https://www.nj.gov/labor/wageandhour/prevailing-rates/public-works/)\n\n[‍](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/purchase/forms.shtml)Some public works projects require surety bonds to bid. Surety bonds make sure subcontractors and suppliers get paid for their work. The New Jersey Economic Development Authority [can help small businesses prepare to apply for surety bonds.](https://www.njeda.com/njbonding/) Contractors can also apply through the U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA)[ Surety Bond Guaranty program.\_](https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/surety-bonds)"
heading-2: State Contracting for Eligible Small Businesses
main-text-2: "### Bidding on State Contracts as a Small Business\n\nFor small businesses, the best way to start working with the State is through set-asides, subcontracting, and small purchase orders. To bid on New Jersey state contracts as a small business:\n\n* Register your business with the state and get any necessary certifications\n* Find a contract to bid on\n* Download the bid documents. Read them carefully to understand the bidding requirements\n* Prepare your bid by providing all requested information\n* Submit your bid by the deadline, following the instructions for submission\n\n### Using NJSTART as a Small Business\n\nMost small businesses work with the state as subcontractors. They usually work with larger companies, called prime contractors, who manage the main contract. Many contracts get renewed rather than replaced with new ones. As a small business, you can use NJSTART to:\n\n* Search for active contracts and see which companies hold them\n* Contact prime contractors and offer your services as subcontractors\n* Find out about new bidding opportunities and identify potential bidders before contracts are awarded\n\n### Small Business Set-Aside Programs\n\nA set-aside program reserves certain State contracts for eligible small businesses. New Jersey has two set-aside programs:\_\n\n* 25% for Small Business Enterprises\n* 3% for Disabled Veteran-Owned Business Enterprises\n\nTo compete for these contracts, you need to [get certified as a Small Business Enterprise (SBE) in New Jersey.](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/revenue/business-cert-program.shtml) Having a SBE certificate may give you an advantage with government contracts. You can qualify in the following categories:\n\n* Small Business Enterprise\n* Minority-Woman Owned Business Enterprise\n* Veteran-Disabled Veteran Owned Business Enterprise\n* LGBTQ+ Owned Business Enterprise\n* Socially and Economically Disadvantaged Business Enterprise\n\nTo certify as a SBE, you must meet certain eligibility requirements:\n\n* Primary location is in New Jersey\n* 100 or fewer full-time employees\n* 51% or more of their business operations/sales in New Jersey, OR has 51% or more of the company’s full-time employees in New Jersey\n* Meets additional revenue limits based on your business activity\n\nApply for a small business certification through the [Uniform Certification Service](https://www.njportal.com/DOR/SBERegistry). Application requirements vary based on type of certification. The [New Jersey Certification Program](https://www.nj.gov/treasury/revenue/ucs.shtml) has information on what you need to apply. Most businesses must provide:\n\n* An EIN\n* Social Security Number\n* An identified NAICS code\n* Information on your business's gross revenue for the last 3 completed tax years\n* Business formation date (for LLC, C corps, LPs and LLPs) You can find this date on your\n* Certificate of Formation\n\nState agencies must also buy 3% of their goods and services from the [Central Nonprofit Agency, ACCSES NJ](https://accsesnj.org/). ACCSES NJ connects customers with organizations that hire people with disabilities. Small businesses can join this program by becoming a member of ACCSES NJ.\n\n### New Jersey Selective Assistance Vendor Information (NJ SAVI)\n\nOnce you certify as a small business, your information is listed in the [Selective Assistance Vendor Information (NJ SAVI)](https://data.nj.gov/stories/s/NJ-SAVI-Access-Story/9k98-6fqb/) database. NJ\_SAVI does not list bidding opportunities. You must actively find opportunities for your business. You can also check your SBE verification and certification status on NJ SAVI.\n\nNJ SAVI helps prime contractors find subcontractors to meet set-aside requirements for certain contracts. Prime contractors use NJ SAVI to check if a subcontractor's certification is still active or to find subcontractors in specific fields. State agencies also use NJ SAVI to confirm certifications for the [two primary set-aside categories](#Small-Business-Set-Aside-Programs).\n\nNJ SAVI can help you get noticed by prime contractors, but you need to reach out to them directly for potential opportunities."

Procurement is the process of purchasing goods and services. heading-4: What to Do Once Awarded a State Contract
main-text-4: If you receive a State contract award, you must complete required forms, certificates, and documentation. heading-5: Contracting with the Federal Government
main-text-5: "Federal agencies must publicly list their contract opportunities, and some contracts are set aside for small businesses. Contracts can be awarded to one vendor (single award) or multiple vendors (multiple award).\n\n[Find federal government contracting opportunities on SAM.gov](http://sam.gov). You can increase your chances of winning a federal contract by [researching the federal marketplace](https://buy.gsa.gov/contracts/home) and [learning how to win contracts from the Small Business Administration (SBA).](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-guide/how-win-contracts)\n\n### Contracting as a Small Business\n\nThe Small Business Administration helps small businesses win federal contracts. [There are several contracts you may be eligible for:](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-guide/types-contracts)\n\n* **Competitive set-aside contracts**: Contracts that limit competition to small businesses. Some set-asides are open to any small business, while others are reserved for those in SBA contracting programs\n* **Sole-source set-aside contracts:** Contracts given directly to one small business without competition, usually based on specific qualifications\n* **Joint ventures:** Small businesses can form a joint venture to pool resources and compete for contract awards. This allows a small business to compete for contracts reserved for service-disabled veteran-owned, women-owned, or HUBZone businesses\n\nThe SBA also has [contracting assistance programs for eligible small businesses](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs). Under this program, small businesses must receive 23% of federal contracting dollars. Some contracts are for small businesses in certain socioeconomic categories, such as:\n\n* Small businesses [owned by socially or economically disadvantaged people](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs/small-disadvantaged-business)\n* The [8(a) Business Development program](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs/8a-business-development-program) for socially and economically disadvantaged businesses who have been in business for two or more years\n* [Women-owned small businesses](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs/women-owned-small-business-federal-contract-program)\n* [Veteran-owned small businesses](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs/veteran-contracting-assistance-programs#id-service-disabled-veteran-owned-small-business-program)\n* [Historically Underutilized Business (HUBZone) program](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-assistance-programs/hubzone-program) for businesses in historically underutilized business zones\n\nParticipating in one of these SBA programs means you’ll have fewer businesses to compete with to win a government contract. You must meet eligibility requirements and certify your business’s socioeconomic status before you can bid on a set-aside contract. Check your eligibility [through MySBA Certifications.](https://certifications.sba.gov/)\_\n\n### Federal Contracting Rules and Requirements\n\nTo join SBA contracting programs, you must[ qualify as a small business according to SBA size standards](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-guide/size-standards). You must also [comply with federal contracting rules and responsibilities](https://www.sba.gov/federal-contracting/contracting-guide/governing-rules-responsibilities)."
